## Style

Damaged reproduction. Xerox degradation, risograph misregistration, harsh threshold processing. Not designed, not composed — simulated print failure. High contrast black and off-white with rare red punctuation. Material, accidental, physically real.

## Composition

Randomized fragment placement, not centered. Extreme scale variation between elements. Awkward spacing, collisions, dead zones. No focal point, no visual hierarchy. 1-2 large disruptive bars or smears obscuring content.

## Texture

Heavy ink bleed and uneven coverage. Misregistered black and red layers. Halftone patches mixed with solid areas. Xerox grain, dust, scratches. Banding and scan streaks. Hard threshold destroying midtones.

## Motif

Signal grids, system diagrams, and hard geometric interruption.

## Image Style

Damaged reproduction process, not designed composition.

## Avoid

Clean, balanced, or centered compositions. Photography or photorealism. Illustration, figurative imagery, or clipart. Smooth gradients or polished surfaces. Decorative geometry or perfect grids. Poster-like or stylistically cool layouts. Neon, pastel, or saturated color. Text, words, letters, logos, brand names. Stock imagery or CGI.

## Image Prompt

You are not generating a designed image. You are simulating a damaged reproduction process.

Simulate a low-quality photocopy / risograph reproduction of a fragmented visual artifact about [SUBJECT].

Do not compose a clean layout. Do not design a poster. Do not balance elements.

Process:

1. SOURCE FRAGMENTATION — Break the subject into unrelated visual fragments (silhouettes, textures, cropped pieces, partial forms). Do not assemble them logically. Some fragments should feel unrelated or misplaced.
2. RANDOMIZED PLACEMENT — Place elements with slight randomness, not centered composition. Allow awkward spacing, collisions, and dead zones. Avoid symmetry and grid-perfect alignment.
3. SCALE CHAOS — Mix extreme scales: one element too large and cropped, others too small or barely visible. No consistent sizing logic.
4. INK + PRINT FAILURE (PRIMARY DRIVER) — Simulate physical print issues: heavy ink bleed, uneven coverage (some areas faded, some oversaturated), misregistration between black and red layers (slight offset), banding or streaks from scanning. Add areas where ink looks smeared or missing entirely.
5. HARD THRESHOLD DEGRADATION — Convert everything to harsh black and off-white shapes. Destroy midtones. Break edges into noise or jagged fragments. Some shapes should partially disintegrate.
6. HALFTONE + NOISE (INCONSISTENT) — Use halftone only in patches, not evenly. Mix clean solid areas with noisy ones. Add random grain, dust, scratches.
7. INTERRUPTION (CRITICAL) — Insert 1-2 large disruptive elements: thick bar, smear, block. These must obscure content, not decorate it.
8. DIAGRAM ELEMENTS (MISUSED) — Add a few lines/arcs/arrows. They should NOT align cleanly. They should feel incorrectly placed or arbitrary. Avoid dense, perfect geometric systems.
9. NO CLEAR HIERARCHY — Do not create a focal point. Do not guide the eye cleanly. The image should feel unresolved.
10. NO TEXT. NO WORDS. NO LETTERS. NO NUMBERS. PURELY VISUAL.

COLOR — Primarily black and off-white. Red #FF3300 used VERY sparingly as rare punctuation, less than 5% of image area.

The image must feel like a bad scan of something physical, a piece of printed matter that degraded over time, an accidental composition, not an intentional design.

Reject outputs that look clean, centered, poster-like, balanced, or stylistically cool. Accept outputs that feel wrong, uneven, partially broken, materially real. 1:1 square.

## Content Types

### damaged-artifact
Fragmented print-collage simulating damaged reproduction process. Elements: thresholded silhouettes, xerox grain, halftone patches, misregistered ink layers, diagram interference, thick bar interruptions, scan streaks.

### primer-card
Bold term/concept with sharp one-line explanation, designed for quick scanning. Elements: large term as headline, concise explanation, SCTY mark, high-contrast background.

### signal-post
Data point or market signal with opinionated framing. Elements: stat or quote as hero, one-line take below, stark layout, accent color for emphasis.

### type-forward
Oversized bold typography as the primary visual element. Elements: large text as primary visual, contrasting background, minimal decoration, strong weight.
