## Style

Agnes Martin. Pale gesso ground with faint pencil lines, horizontal color washes, meditative repetition, near-monochrome warmth. Hand-drawn imperfection within geometric order. Quiet, not empty. Calm editorial restraint.

## Composition

Horizontal bands of varying width. Pencil grid barely visible beneath wash. Large open field with subtle internal rhythm. Quiet asymmetry in spacing. Content floats, edges breathe.

## Texture

Gesso tooth on canvas. Graphite pencil lines. Dilute acrylic wash. Paper grain. Slight hand-drawn unevenness.

## Motif

Horizontal bands and pencil grids on warm pale grounds.

## Image Style

Agnes Martin — meditative abstraction, not representation.

## Avoid

Photography or photorealism. Illustration, clipart, or figurative imagery. Objects, people, hands, faces, scenes. Bold saturated color. Text, signage, logos, brand names. Stock imagery or CGI. Busy or crowded compositions. Glossy or polished surfaces. Neon, purple, magenta. Symbols, diagrams, icons.

## Image Prompt

An abstract painting in the style of Agnes Martin. Pale warm ground (cream, ivory, or warm white gesso). Faint horizontal bands of dilute warm color — ochre, sandstone, pale terracotta, or muted rust. Subtle pencil grid lines barely visible beneath the wash, hand-drawn with slight irregularity. The bands should vary slightly in width and spacing — not mechanical, not perfect. Feeling: meditative, quiet, sustained attention. Like counting without arriving. [SUBJECT] should influence the rhythm — tighter bands suggest tension, wider spacing suggests openness. No objects, no figures, no scenes, no symbols, no text, no logos. Pure abstraction. Warm near-monochrome. Paper or canvas texture visible. 1:1 square.

## Content Types

### hero-stat
Single dominant number or short phrase, very large, bold serif, minimal supporting text.

### fact-list
Eyebrow label + 3-4 short facts stacked, clean sans-serif.

### quote
Pillar perspective or expert quote, serif italic, generous margins.

### bold-statement
Short punchy phrase filling the frame, all-caps or large mixed-case.
